Oregon Memorial Day Escape

A boutique-style road trip through Willamette Valley wine country and the dramatic Oregon coast — designed around vineyard tastings, dune sunsets, rugged coastline, scenic drives, and memorable restaurants.

The Route

This itinerary is intentionally structured to minimize backtracking while maximizing scenery and atmosphere. You’ll spend almost no meaningful time in Portland itself, instead using it only as your arrival gateway before diving immediately into wine country.

The pacing gradually evolves from sophisticated vineyard tastings and countryside roads into rugged Pacific coastline, dramatic dune landscapes, charming coastal towns, and iconic Oregon sunsets.

“The best Oregon trips balance world-class wine with wild Pacific scenery.”

Wednesday • May 20

Arrival in Portland — easy airport night before heading directly into wine country.

Arrival Strategy

Landing at 9:38 PM and staying near PDX is absolutely the correct move. It avoids a late-night drive into wine country and allows you to start the trip refreshed the next morning.

Your Holiday Inn Express near the airport keeps logistics simple while giving you quick access to the rental facility first thing in the morning.

Optional Late-Night Stops

Migration Brewing — excellent Oregon craft beer list with a relaxed atmosphere.

Screen Door Eastside — iconic Southern-inspired comfort food if you arrive hungry.

Loyal Legion — massive Pacific Northwest beer selection.

Thursday • May 21

Willamette Valley Wine Country — vineyard views, rolling hills, and your strongest tasting day.

8:00 AM — Pick Up Rental SUV Your Mazda CX-50 is perfect for this trip: comfortable highway cruiser, scenic-drive friendly, and ideal for the coast.
8:30 AM — Breakfast at Screen Door Eastside One of Portland’s most iconic breakfasts. Famous for praline bacon, biscuits, fried chicken, and hearty brunch plates.
10:00 AM — Elk Cove Vineyards Elk Cove feels quintessentially Oregon wine country: rolling vineyard hills, quiet countryside roads, and a more intimate atmosphere than Napa-style wineries. The Vine-to-Wine experience gives you a deeper introduction to Oregon Pinot Noir.
12:15 PM — Lunch at Red Hills Market One of the best all-around wine-country lunch spots. Excellent sandwiches, wood-fired pizza, salads, beer, and outdoor seating.
1:00 PM — Fairsing Vineyard Arguably the prettiest vineyard setting of the trip. Elevated hillside views stretch across layers of vineyards and Oregon farmland.
3:00 PM — Soter Vineyards Mineral Springs Ranch feels luxurious but understated. Beautiful ranch property, thoughtful hospitality, and expansive valley scenery.

Evening in McMinnville

Downtown McMinnville is one of the most enjoyable small-town wine destinations in America. Walkable streets, tasting rooms, breweries, restaurants, and boutique storefronts create a relaxed but upscale atmosphere.

Best Dinner: Hayward — refined Pacific Northwest farm-to-table cuisine.
Most Fun: Pura Vida Cocina — lively Latin-inspired coastal dishes and margaritas.
Best Brewery: Heater Allen Brewing — internationally respected lagers.

Friday • May 22

Dundee Hills tastings → Pacific City sunset dunes.

Dusky Goose

Dusky Goose offers one of the more polished and elegant Dundee Hills tasting experiences. The setting feels elevated and relaxed at the same time, with expansive vineyard views and refined Pinot Noir.

Stoller Family Estate

Stoller is among Oregon’s most photogenic wineries. Rolling hills, modern architecture, and a broad open property make it one of the most visually memorable stops in the valley.

Cape Kiwanda Sunset

This is one of the signature moments of the entire trip.

Cape Kiwanda’s massive dune rises directly above the Pacific Ocean, creating one of the most dramatic sunset environments on the Oregon coast. Climbing the dune is short but steep — and completely worth it.

After sunset, Pelican Brewing sits directly on the beach with beer, seafood, and one of the best casual atmospheres in the state.

Saturday • May 23

Central Oregon Coast exploration — Newport, Yachats, and Cape Perpetua.

This is your “wild Oregon” day. The polished vineyard atmosphere gives way to rugged cliffs, crashing waves, dramatic viewpoints, dune landscapes, and iconic Highway 101 coastal driving.

Newport

Newport feels like a real working Oregon coast town rather than purely a tourist stop. The harbor has fishing boats, seafood restaurants, breweries, and classic coastal energy.

Best Lunch: Local Ocean Seafoods — likely the best seafood meal of the trip.

Yachats & Cape Perpetua

This area is widely considered the most dramatic scenery on the Oregon coast.

Thor’s Well, Spouting Horn, and the Cape Perpetua overlook combine crashing surf, cliffs, forests, and huge Pacific views.

Best Hikes & Scenic Stops

Thor’s Well: short walk with enormous payoff.
Cape Perpetua Lookout: dramatic elevated Pacific views.
Devil’s Punchbowl: unique volcanic coastal formation.
Depoe Bay: whale sightings and dramatic harbor views.

Sunday • May 24

Northbound coast drive → Cannon Beach finale.

Today combines the best of Oregon’s northern coast: forested headlands, dramatic viewpoints, charming beach towns, and your iconic Cannon Beach finale.

Cape Lookout

One of the best easy-to-moderate hikes on the coast. Forested trails open into spectacular Pacific viewpoints.

Neahkahnie Mountain Viewpoint

Perhaps the single best roadside viewpoint on the Oregon coast. The sweeping coastline below feels cinematic.

Cannon Beach Finale

Cannon Beach is touristy for a reason — it’s stunning.

Haystack Rock at sunset creates one of the classic Pacific Northwest landscapes. Ecola State Park adds dramatic forested cliffs and ocean viewpoints just north of town.

Best Dinner: The Wayfarer Restaurant — ocean views and refined coastal dining.
Best Brewery: Public Coast Brewing.

Have an Incredible Trip

Oregon in late May is at its best: green landscapes, dramatic coastline, cool evenings, and long golden sunsets.
