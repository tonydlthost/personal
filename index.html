Oregon Memorial Day Escape

A boutique-style road trip through Willamette Valley wine country and the Oregon coast — built around confirmed reservations, scenic timing, vineyard views, dune sunsets, breweries, and relaxed coastal exploring.

The Route

This itinerary is structured to keep Portland to a minimum and move quickly into the best parts of the trip: Willamette Valley wine country, Pacific City’s dune-and-ocean scenery, Newport’s working-harbor seafood scene, and a Cannon Beach finale.

The biggest win in your updated plan is sleeping in Pacific City for two nights. That gives you a true coastal base, keeps Saturday flexible, and lets the trip feel less like a point-to-point sprint.

“Wine country first, coast second, Cannon Beach finale — the cleanest version of this Oregon weekend.”
Night 1Portland Airport
Night 2Downtown McMinnville
Nights 3–4Pacific City
Night 5Cannon Beach
Full trip driving overview: PDX → McMinnville → Pacific City → Cannon Beach → PDX.

Arrival in Portland — an easy airport night before wine country.

Evening Plan

Flight9:38 PM — Arrive at PDXLand in Portland and keep the night simple. Since you arrive late, the best move is avoiding any meaningful driving tonight.
Hotel10:15–10:45 PM — Check into Holiday Inn Express Portland AirportStaying near PDX keeps this night purely functional and lets you start fresh the next morning.

If You Want a Quick Bite

Migration Brewing is a good casual Oregon beer option if you want one low-effort drink and snack after landing.

Loyal Legion is a better beer-geek choice if you want a large Oregon tap list.

Recommendation: only go out if you are genuinely hungry. The trip gets good immediately the next morning.

Day 0 drive: PDX arrival → airport hotel.

Thursday • May 21

Portland breakfast, rental car pickup, and a full scenic wine-country day.

Rental Car8:00 AM — Pick up Budget Intermediate SUV at PDXBudget confirmation #45638768US0. Pick up at Portland International Airport. The Mazda CX-50 or similar is a great fit for wine-country roads and coastal driving.
Breakfast8:30–9:15 AM — Screen Door EastsideStart with Portland’s best classic breakfast energy before leaving the city. It is famous for hearty Southern-inspired brunch plates, biscuits, praline bacon, and fried chicken. If you want something quicker, Pine State Biscuits is the faster, more casual alternative.
Winery10:00 AM–12:00 PM — Elk Cove VineyardsConfirmed: Vine to Wine Experience, party of 2, confirmation #TOCK-R-0OEG6UPI. Elk Cove is a beautiful first stop because it feels rural, green, and unmistakably Willamette Valley. The experience includes a provisions board and gives you a deeper sense of how the wines connect to the vineyard landscape.
Lunch12:20–12:50 PM — Quick lunch / provisions stopBecause Fairsing is at 1:00 PM, keep lunch efficient. Best option: grab something light at or near Carlton/Yamhill, or rely on Elk Cove’s provisions board and save a fuller meal for dinner. Red Hills Market is great but slightly tight with this reservation spacing.
Winery1:00–2:15 PM — Fairsing VineyardConfirmed: Wine Flight Tasting, reservation #3380. Fairsing is one of the prettiest settings in the trip: hillside views, forested edges, vineyard rows, and a more peaceful, estate-like feel. Pack an extra layer — the winery specifically warns that Oregon weather can shift quickly.
Winery3:00–4:15 PM — Soter VineyardsConfirmed: Estates Experience, confirmation #TOCK-R-W0MCV3R3. Soter’s Mineral Springs Ranch is polished and scenic, with wide valley views and a more curated tasting feel. Arrive on time — they note that guests more than 10 minutes late may need to reschedule.
Stay5:00 PM — Check into Rory’s Rest at First and FordConfirmed Airbnb: 605 NE 1st St, McMinnville. Check-in after 3:00 PM; checkout Friday by 11:00 AM. This location is excellent because you can park the car and walk downtown for dinner and drinks.
Dinner7:00 PM — McMinnville dinnerBest splurge: Hayward, a refined Pacific Northwest farm-to-table restaurant. Mid-range/fun: Pura Vida Cocina for bright Latin-inspired food and drinks. Beer stop: Heater Allen Brewing for excellent lagers.

Why This Day Works

This is your most reservation-heavy day, so the key is pacing. Elk Cove is immersive, Fairsing is scenic, and Soter is polished. By ending in walkable downtown McMinnville, you avoid any evening driving after a full wine day.

Day 1 driving route: airport hotel → breakfast → Elk Cove → Fairsing → Soter → McMinnville Airbnb.

Friday • May 22

Dundee Hills tastings, then west to Pacific City for sunset.

Coffee8:30 AM — Flag & Wire CoffeeStart the morning with one of McMinnville’s better coffee stops. Keep breakfast light since you have two tastings and a coastal drive ahead.
Checkout10:15–10:30 AM — Leave McMinnville AirbnbOfficial checkout is by 11:00 AM, but leaving a bit earlier gives you a relaxed buffer before Dusky Goose.
Winery11:00 AM–12:15 PM — Dusky GooseConfirmed: Estate Tasting, party of 2, confirmation #TOCK-R-UMJ33J43. Dusky Goose is a polished Dundee Hills tasting room with elegant Pinot Noir and a quiet, refined feel. It is a good contrast to the more expansive ranch atmosphere of Soter the day before.
Winery12:45–2:00 PM — Stoller Family EstateConfirmed updated reservation: Classic Tasting, party of 2, confirmation #TOCK-R-M3E3LGH3. Stoller is one of Oregon’s most photogenic wine properties: broad rolling hills, modern tasting architecture, and a bright, open estate setting. Their reminder about Friday/weekend traffic is worth taking seriously, so do not cut the arrival close.
Lunch2:15–3:00 PM — Red Hills Market or Trellis DundeeAfter Stoller, grab lunch before the coast drive. Red Hills Market is better if you want casual, quick, and reliable. Trellis Dundee is better if you want a more composed sit-down wine-country lunch.
Drive3:15–5:00 PM — Drive to Pacific CityThis is a beautiful transition drive: vineyard hills fade into forest and eventually open into the coastal landscape. Aim to arrive close to check-in rather than pushing dinner late.
Stay5:00 PM — Check into Short Board StudioConfirmed Airbnb: 32742 Cape Kiwanda Dr, Pacific City. Check-in after 5:00 PM; checkout Sunday by 11:00 AM. This is a great coastal base because you are right by Cape Kiwanda.
Sunset6:15–7:45 PM — Cape Kiwanda dune + beach walkThis is the signature sunset of the trip. The dune climb is steep but short, and the payoff is huge: sandstone cliffs, ocean views, surfers, and golden light over the Pacific.
Dinner7:45–8:30 PM — Pelican Brewing Pacific CityGo for the setting as much as the food. It is right on the beach, making it the easiest and most atmospheric dinner after Cape Kiwanda.

Pacific City Arrival

Moving to Pacific City tonight is a major upgrade. Instead of waking up inland and burning Saturday morning on the drive, you get a beach sunset Friday and a much more relaxed coastal day Saturday.

Day 2 driving route: McMinnville Airbnb → Dusky Goose → Stoller → lunch → Pacific City Airbnb → Pelican Brewing.

Saturday • May 23

Pacific City to Newport — coastal stops, seafood, viewpoints, and a relaxed return.

This is a balanced central-coast day built around scenic pull-offs, a relaxed pace, Newport seafood, and some of the best quick-payoff viewpoints along Highway 101.

Coffee8:30 AM — Stimulus Coffee + BakeryEasy local coffee before heading south. If the weather is clear, take a quick beach walk before getting in the car.
Nature9:15–10:15 AM — Neskowin Beach / Proposal RockA mellow first stop with a broad beach, moody sea stack views, and a different feel from Pacific City. It is an easy way to start the day without committing to a full hike.
Scenic Stop10:45–11:15 AM — Boiler Bay State Scenic ViewpointA quick but dramatic ocean viewpoint north of Depoe Bay. Great for crashing waves and a short leg stretch.
Town Stop11:20–11:50 AM — Depoe BayA tiny harbor town known for whale-watching viewpoints and wave action. Keep this short unless you spot whales or want to linger around the seawall.
Geology Stop12:05–12:30 PM — Devil’s Punchbowl State Natural AreaA compact and unique coastal formation. It is a great payoff for minimal time: park, look down into the collapsed sea cave, take a few photos, and continue south.
Lunch1:00–2:15 PM — Local Ocean Seafoods, NewportThis is the recommended main meal of the day. Local Ocean feels like the right restaurant for Newport: fresh seafood, harbor atmosphere, and a menu that fits the working-coast setting.
Explore2:15–3:30 PM — Newport BayfrontWalk the historic bayfront, watch the boats, browse shops, and enjoy the busier working-harbor energy. This gives the day a town component instead of only viewpoint hopping.
Optional3:45–4:45 PM — Yaquina Head Outstanding Natural AreaIf you still have energy, this is the best Newport-area nature add-on: lighthouse views, ocean cliffs, seabirds, and tidepool potential depending on tide timing.
Return5:00–6:45 PM — Drive back to Pacific CityHead back before it gets too late. You already had your big seafood lunch, so the evening can stay simple.
Dinner7:15 PM — Casual dinner in Pacific CityBest repeat if you loved it: Pelican Brewing. Alternative: Meridian Restaurant at Headlands Lodge for a more elevated dinner, or grab casual food and enjoy the beach.

The Ideal Central Coast Day

This stretch of the Oregon coast delivers exactly what makes Highway 101 memorable: dramatic wave action, small harbor towns, sea cliffs, hidden viewpoints, and some of the best seafood in the Pacific Northwest — all while keeping the pace relaxed enough to genuinely enjoy the stops instead of rushing through them.

Best Hike / Walk Options

Easy: Neskowin Beach and Proposal Rock.

Scenic: Yaquina Head lighthouse area.

Very short: Devil’s Punchbowl viewpoint.

Food & Beer Notes

Local Ocean Seafoods should be the anchor meal. It is the place that most strongly fits your route and timing.

Rogue Ales is nearby if you want a quick brewery stop in Newport, but do not force it if you would rather spend time outside.

Day 3 driving route: Pacific City → Neskowin → Boiler Bay → Depoe Bay → Devil’s Punchbowl → Newport → optional Yaquina Head → Pacific City.

Sunday • May 24

North coast drive to Cannon Beach — viewpoints, cheese, beach town finale.

Checkout10:00–10:30 AM — Check out of Short Board StudioOfficial checkout is by 11:00 AM. Leaving around 10:00 keeps the day relaxed while still giving you enough time for scenic stops before Cannon Beach.
Hike10:50 AM–12:00 PM — Cape Lookout State ParkThis is your best northbound coastal hike option. Do a shortened out-and-back rather than committing to the full trail. You get forest, ocean air, and a more rugged headland feel without overloading the day.
Lunch / Stop12:45–1:45 PM — Tillamook CreameryYes, it is touristy. It is also an efficient, fun stop with cheese, ice cream, and an easy break from the drive. Treat it as a casual lunch and leg stretch.
Viewpoint2:35–2:55 PM — Neahkahnie Mountain ViewpointOne of the best roadside views on the entire Oregon coast. Do not skip this if the weather is clear — the coastline view is cinematic.
Town Stop3:05–3:45 PM — ManzanitaA quieter, more local-feeling beach town than Cannon Beach. Great for a short beach walk or coffee before the final push north.
Hotel4:00 PM — Check into The Waves, Cannon BeachConfirmed: The Waves, 188 W 2nd St, Cannon Beach. Flagship King Studio, confirmation #1261796. Check-in is 4:00 PM; check-out Monday by 11:00 AM.
Sunset6:30–8:00 PM — Haystack Rock beach walkThis is the classic Cannon Beach moment. Walk the beach at golden hour and sunset, then wander town before dinner.
Dinner8:00 PM — Cannon Beach dinnerBest scenic/upscale choice: The Wayfarer Restaurant. Beer-focused casual choice: Public Coast Brewing. More relaxed dinner: Castaways.

Cannon Beach Finale

Cannon Beach is polished and popular, but it earns it. Haystack Rock at sunset is one of the defining Oregon coast views, and staying at The Waves puts you exactly where you want to be for an easy final evening.

Day 4 driving route: Pacific City → Cape Lookout → Tillamook Creamery → Neahkahnie → Manzanita → The Waves → Haystack Rock.

Have an Incredible Trip

Oregon in late May should be green, dramatic, cool, and atmospheric — exactly the right backdrop for wine country, coastal drives, dune sunsets, and a Cannon Beach finale.
